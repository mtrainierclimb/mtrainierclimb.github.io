About the Climb

The four of us will be climbing Mount Rainier to raise awareness for Alzheimer's. This climb will be a challenging and rewarding experience, but it is also a way to bring attention to a cause that is important to us.

  • Tallest mountain in the U.S. state of Washington and the Cascades.
  • Summit Height: 14,417 ft

The climb will take place on May 11th and is expected to last several days. The team will be hiking to the summit of Mount Rainier, which is the highest peak in Washington State. Along the way, they will face steep terrain, extreme weather conditions, and other challenges that come with climbing a mountain.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is Alzheimer's?

    Alzheimer's is a progressive disease that affects memory, thinking, and behavior. It is the most common cause of dementia, and it currently has no cure. As the disease progresses, it can severely impact a person's ability to perform daily tasks and communicate effectively.

  • Why are you climbing Mt. Rainier?

    We are climbing Mt. Rainier to raise awareness about Alzheimer's disease and to raise funds for Alzheimer's research and advocacy. Our goal is to highlight the importance of early detection and treatment, and to encourage others to support this cause.

  • How can I support the climb?

    You can support our climb by making a donation to our fundraising campaign. All donations will go directly to Alzheimer's research and advocacy organizations, and every dollar counts in the fight against this devastating disease.

  • What impact will my donation have?

    Your donation will make a significant impact in the fight against Alzheimer's disease. It will help to fund research into new treatments and potential cures, as well as advocacy efforts to improve access to care and support for those affected by the disease.

  • What precautions are you taking to ensure safety during the climb?

    We are taking all necessary precautions to ensure our safety during the climb, including working with experienced guides, following proper climbing procedures, and ensuring that we have the necessary equipment and supplies. We are committed to taking all necessary measures to ensure a safe and successful climb.
